I am a PhD student at Laboratoire de Physique Subatomique et de Cosmologie (LPSC) in Grenoble, France. My supervisor is Pr. Aurélien Barrau, a group member of both LSST and AMS experiments, and Head of the Master in Subatomique and Astroparticle Physics at Grenoble University, which I graduated from in June 2014, within the Master Open course programme of the École Normale Supérieure de Lyon (ENS Lyon).

I am a fellow of ENS Lyon since 2009. This prestigeous instutions is currently funding my PhD, after it had provided me with a genereous scolarship for the whole duration of my undergraduate studies (except for the first two years that I completed in Lycée Louis-le-Grand, in Paris, funded by a grant from association Promotion des Talents).

I am a recipient of the Diplôme de l'ENS de Lyon, an honorary degree that is granted to students who have gone abroad for a substantial amount of time and have opened their formation towards different topics than their main subject.
In 2010, I followed a one year course on British Postmodern Art. In 2012, I worked for six months at the Science and Technology Department of the French Embassy in India (New Delhi). And more recently in 2014, I did a six months internship at the University of Manchester (UK), under the supervision of Pr. Richard Battye, working on f(R) gravity.
